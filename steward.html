DELEGATE PLATFORM | STEWARD PLATFORM


If elected to the position of working group steward, I will prioritize accomplishing these changes during my tenure, or at the very least continue to present and advocate for them in governance discussion.

1. Secure .eth TLD

Create a task force with the express purpose of establishing a formal partnership with the necessary parties (government of Ethiopia, ICANN, IANA) to negotiate obtaining rights to manage or operate the .eth TLD.

This task force should report progress updates two times per month for inclusion in the ENS DAO Newsletter on the 1st and 15th.


2. Second Airdrop

Every wallet that held at least one .eth name throughout the entire period of 1 November 2021 - 31 December 2022 is eligible to claim 1 $ENS token.

Addresses that are set as a primary name are eligible to claim an additional 1 $ENS.

The original airdrop interface and mechanism should be able to be repurposed to facilitate this at claim.ens.domains

This can also be used as an opportunity to push for redelegation.


3. Quadratic Voting

Weight delegates $ENS vote based on the square root of their voting power. Voting power in this context refers to the the total amount of $ENS delegated to an individual plus the amount of $ENS held in their wallet.

weighted vote = square root($ENS in wallet + delegated votes)

A delegate with a voting power of 1 has 1 weighted vote.
A delegate with a voting power of 4 has 2 weighted votes.
A delegate with a voting power of 9 has 3 weighted votes.
and so on

This gives more voice to community members with smaller amounts of $ENS in their wallets (or delegated to them), ensuring that proposals supported by many individual community members are not overlooked in favor of those supported by a few.

This also exponentially decreases the effectiveness of simply buying more votes.


4. Reconsider Delegation Process

Consider implementing an expiration framework for votes delegated to inactive delegates.

For much the same reasons that ownership of .eth names should not be permanent, the same logic should apply to delegation.
